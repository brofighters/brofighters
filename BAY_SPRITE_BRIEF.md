# Bay Sprite Brief

This is the production sprite brief for the Bro Fighters `Bay` fighter slot.
It turns the supplied likeness photo into a game-ready template for the Businessman of Azeroth.

Use this together with [SPRITE_PRODUCTION.md](/Users/alexyap/Documents/Codex/2026-06-18/github-plugin-github-openai-curated-remote/work/brofighters/SPRITE_PRODUCTION.md).

## Identity

- **Roster name:** Bay
- **Role:** Businessman of Azeroth
- **Fight fantasy:** calm pressure, confident forward momentum, high-conviction all-in offense
- **Read at a glance:** solid face, steady poker look, businesswear silhouette, gold coin detail

## Likeness Markers

These should survive even at small sprite size:

1. **Face shape:** broad, grounded, steady proportions.
2. **Expression:** unreadable poker face or faint knowing half-smile.
3. **Hair:** short dark hair with a simple neat shape.
4. **Build:** medium, sturdy, grounded rather than skinny.
5. **Hands:** visible and deliberate, because the photo already gives a tabletop poker energy.

If detail must be simplified, keep the face shape and business silhouette before smaller trim details.

## Sprite Construction

- **Frame size:** 96 x 128 px
- **Visible character height:** about 87 px
- **Facing:** draw facing right
- **Origin:** bottom center between feet
- **Head proportion:** slightly oversized for readability
- **Shoulders:** broader than Yap, but not tank-like
- **Stance:** stable and slightly forward, like he is always leaning into the trade

## Palette Direction

- **Hair:** deep brown-black
- **Skin:** warm medium skin tone
- **Businesswear base:** charcoal, navy, and off-white
- **Accent:** gold `#ffbd59`
- **Coin/chip detail:** polished gold with a brighter rim
- **Fantasy trim:** subtle muted bronze or deep steel-blue
- **Outline:** dark navy-brown, not pure black

Keep the palette rich and clean, not flashy casino-neon.

## Pose Language

Bay should feel composed and expensive.

- Idle should feel like he is reading the room.
- Walk should feel deliberate and self-assured.
- Punches should look forceful but economical.
- Special should feel like commitment and acceleration, not chaotic magic spam.

## Frame-By-Frame Plan

### Idle 1

- Weight slightly forward.
- Front hand near midline, ready.
- Back hand relaxed with coin/chip possibility.
- Face calm and hard to read.

### Idle 2

- Tiny shoulder shift.
- Chin angle changes by 1 px.
- Vest or jacket opening moves subtly.

### Walk 1

- Front foot planted with authority.
- Rear foot driving through.
- Torso slightly committed forward.

### Walk 2

- Passing pose.
- Arms move with confidence, not bounce.
- Keep the business silhouette tidy.

### Walk 3

- Reverse of Walk 1.
- Strong planted feel should remain.

### Walk 4

- Reverse of Walk 2.
- Minimal head bob.

### Jump Rise

- Knees tuck modestly.
- Jacket or shirt hem lifts slightly.
- Hands stay controlled.

### Jump Fall

- Legs extend for a balanced landing.
- One hand can open slightly for control.

### Punch 1A

- Short jab startup.
- Front shoulder turns in.
- Direct and efficient.

### Punch 1B

- Jab extension.
- Clean straight line.
- Optional tiny gold flick or chip-glint near the fist.

### Punch 2A

- Cross startup.
- Hips rotate more clearly.
- Rear shoulder comes forward.

### Punch 2B

- Cross impact.
- This should feel like the strongest grounded contact in the combo.

### Punch 3A

- Finisher startup with a decisive half-step.
- Upper body winds with intent.

### Punch 3B

- Finisher impact with a shoulder-driven feel.
- Can hint at the rush identity even before the special.

### Block

- Tight guard.
- Elbows in, shoulders up slightly.
- Calm, disciplined defense.

### Hit

- Torso recoils.
- Poker face cracks briefly.
- One foot shifts back.

### Knockdown Fall

- Heavy sideways drop.
- Keep the silhouette readable and broad.

### Knockdown Grounded

- One shoulder down, one knee bent.
- Hair and face shape should still be identifiable.

### Special 1: Commit

- Forward lean.
- Coin or chip hand tightens.
- Feet plant for a burst.

### Special 2: Rush Start

- Shoulder drives forward.
- Gold streak begins.
- Coin/chip can appear spinning in the effect.

### Special 3: Rush Impact

- Full committed dash-contact frame.
- Gold speed line and one clean coin spin cue.
- Keep the Warcraft vibe suggestive, not copied.

### Special 4: Recover

- Momentum settles.
- Jacket or shirt edge returns into place.
- Regain composed stance quickly.

## Special Effect Rules

`All-In Rush` should feel rich and forceful.

- Use **gold speed streaks**, not fire trails.
- Add **one clear spinning coin or chip motif**.
- Keep the effect **compact and directional**.
- Avoid literal Warcraft iconography, copied logos, or huge fantasy armor bursts.

## Select Portrait Direction

After the sprite sheet is approved, draw a 512 x 512 portrait with:

- three-quarter pose
- calm poker expression
- one hand flipping a coin or chip
- subtle businesswear layering
- restrained gold accent glow

## Approval Checklist

Approve the sprite only if these all hold true:

1. He reads as calm and dangerous rather than loud or goofy.
2. The face shape preserves Bay’s likeness.
3. The businesswear silhouette is clear at small size.
4. The special reads as an all-in shoulder rush, not generic magic.
5. The coin/chip cue is visible without becoming a gimmick.
