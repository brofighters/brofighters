# Lossy Sprite Brief

This is the production sprite brief for the Bro Fighters `Lossy` fighter slot.
It turns the supplied likeness reference into a game-ready template for the Family-Office Protector.

Use this together with [SPRITE_PRODUCTION.md](/Users/alexyap/Documents/Codex/2026-06-18/github-plugin-github-openai-curated-remote/work/brofighters/SPRITE_PRODUCTION.md).

## Identity

- **Roster name:** Lossy
- **Role:** Family-Office Protector
- **Fight fantasy:** calm defense, quiet authority, protective space control
- **Read at a glance:** cap, glasses, warm smile, understated clothing, soft shield energy

## Likeness Markers

These should survive even at small sprite size:

1. **Cap:** light baseball cap with a simple front mark.
2. **Glasses:** rectangular dark frames.
3. **Smile:** broad, easy, friendly smile.
4. **Face shape:** gentle rounded jaw and approachable expression.
5. **Build:** medium, secure, stable, not flashy.

If detail must be simplified, preserve the cap, glasses, and smile first.

## Sprite Construction

- **Frame size:** 96 x 128 px
- **Visible character height:** about 86-87 px
- **Facing:** draw facing right
- **Origin:** bottom center between feet
- **Head proportion:** slightly oversized for readability
- **Hands:** readable open-palm shapes are important for the protector theme
- **Stance:** closed, centered, and stable

## Palette Direction

- **Cap:** warm off-white or pale cream
- **Glasses:** charcoal-black
- **Base clothing:** charcoal, muted olive-gray, or quiet dark neutral
- **Accent:** dusty pink `#f29de1`
- **Metal detail:** subtle rose-gold
- **Shield effect:** translucent blush-pink and pale silver
- **Outline:** muted charcoal-brown, not pure black

Keep the overall palette soft, tasteful, and low-ego.

## Pose Language

Lossy should feel secure rather than aggressive.

- Idle should feel composed and inwardly guarded.
- Walk should be measured and economical.
- Punches should be compact, surprisingly effective, but not wild.
- Special should read as protection first, pushback second.

## Frame-By-Frame Plan

### Idle 1

- Weight centered.
- One hand near the centerline.
- Other hand relaxed but ready.
- Smile can be subtle rather than huge in gameplay scale.

### Idle 2

- Small breath or shoulder shift.
- Cap brim angle changes by 1 px if useful.
- Hands stay close and controlled.

### Walk 1

- Front foot planted firmly.
- Rear foot pushes smoothly.
- Torso stays stable.

### Walk 2

- Passing pose.
- Minimal bounce.
- Protector energy should still feel calm.

### Walk 3

- Reverse of Walk 1.
- Stable and unhurried.

### Walk 4

- Reverse of Walk 2.
- Keep the silhouette compact.

### Jump Rise

- Knees tuck modestly.
- Elbows come inward.
- Cap silhouette must stay readable.

### Jump Fall

- Legs extend for a careful landing.
- One hand opens for balance.

### Punch 1A

- Small lead-hand startup.
- Shoulder rotates in.
- Tight and direct.

### Punch 1B

- Jab extension.
- Short, efficient contact.
- Could use a tiny pink glint if needed.

### Punch 2A

- Rear-hand startup.
- Hips rotate with a compact turn.

### Punch 2B

- Rear-hand impact.
- Solid but not flashy.

### Punch 3A

- Finisher startup with a guarded step.
- Off-hand stays close to protect the centerline.

### Punch 3B

- Finisher impact with a small outward push feel.
- Hint at protector force rather than brute rage.

### Block

- Strongest signature defensive pose.
- Forearms close, elbows in.
- One open palm can imply redirecting force.

### Hit

- Torso recoils.
- Smile disappears briefly.
- Cap and glasses remain readable.

### Knockdown Fall

- Controlled sideways drop.
- Keep the silhouette clean and compact.

### Knockdown Grounded

- One arm bracing.
- One knee bent.
- Cap and glasses should still identify him.

### Special 1: Guard Raise

- Hands come inward and forward.
- Body closes protectively.
- First translucent shield panels appear.

### Special 2: Trust Form

- Shield panels interlock more clearly.
- Body stays centered.
- No explosive motion yet.

### Special 3: Push Release

- Short outward burst from the shield.
- Arms open slightly.
- This is the release frame.

### Special 4: Reset

- Shields fade.
- Hands return near the centerline.
- Calm control is restored immediately.

## Special Effect Rules

`Family Trust` should feel elegant and protective.

- Use **interlocking translucent panels**, not giant walls.
- Keep the effect **close to the body**.
- Favor **dusty pink, pale silver, and faint rose-gold**.
- Avoid money rain, cartoon cash, or loud luxury tropes.

## Select Portrait Direction

After the sprite sheet is approved, draw a 512 x 512 portrait with:

- three-quarter angle
- cap and glasses clearly visible
- warm smile preserved
- one palm forward in protection
- shield shimmer framing the body subtly

## Approval Checklist

Approve the sprite only if these all hold true:

1. The cap, glasses, and smile instantly read as Lossy.
2. He feels calm and protective instead of flashy rich.
3. The special reads as shielded trust, not generic magic.
4. The stance feels centered and safe.
5. He has a distinct identity from Xianbin despite both being wealth-coded.
